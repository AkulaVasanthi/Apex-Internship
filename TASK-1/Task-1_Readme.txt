💰 Interactive Budget Tracker: User Guide
Welcome to your personal finance dashboard! This tool helps you keep a real-time eye on your money by tracking what’s coming in (Income) and what’s going out (Expenses).

🚀 How to Get Started
1. View Your Summary
At the very top, you’ll see three main numbers:

Available Balance: This is the "big picture." It’s how much money you have left after subtracting your expenses from your income.

Income (Green): The total of all the money you’ve added.

Expenses (Red): The total of all the money you’ve spent.

2. Record a Transaction
To add a new entry to your tracker, use the Record Transaction form:

Description: Type in what the money was for (e.g., "Monthly Salary," "Grocery Shopping," or "Coffee").

Amount: * To add Income: Just type the number (e.g., 5000).

To add an Expense: Put a minus sign before the number (e.g., -50).

Click "Record Transaction": Your balance and history will update instantly!

3. Review Your History
Scroll down to the History section to see a list of everything you've recorded.

Each item shows the name, the date, and the amount.

Income appears with a green plus sign (+).

Expenses appear with a red minus sign (-).

4. Correcting Mistakes
If you added something by accident:

Hover your mouse (or tap) over the transaction in the History list.

A small red "Del" button will appear on the right.

Click it to remove that item. Your balance will automatically recalculate.
